# US-05 · Combined document and market data answers

**Priority:** P0 · **Story points:** 2

## Story

> As an investor, I want to ask questions that span both company documents and market data so that I can understand how published results relate to share price movements.

## Acceptance criteria (verbatim from `Candidate_04_User_Stories.docx`)

1. The assistant can answer a question that requires both document evidence and market data in a single response. For example: "What did management say about revenue in the FY24 results and how did the share price move in the following quarter?"
2. The document portion of the answer cites its source.
3. The market data portion of the answer references the specific dates and figures used.
4. If one source is available but the other is not, the assistant answers with what it has and states what is missing.
5. The combined answer reads as a coherent response, not two disconnected fragments.

## Why this matters

This is the showcase scenario named in the Candidate Brief: "what management said about revenue in the 2024 annual report and how the share price reacted over the following quarter." It is also the strongest signal that the tool-use loop is working as a genuine router rather than a hand-rolled classifier.

## Definition of done

- No code change should be required to `core/prices.py` or `core/retrieval.py`. This story is about proving the Anthropic tool-use loop in `core/assistant.py` can call **both** tools in a single turn and weave the results into a coherent answer.
- The system prompt in `core/llm.py` instructs the model to decide which tool(s) are needed per question, to call them in any order, and to produce a single integrated answer with distinct inline citation markers for each evidence type.
- When only one tool returns useful results, the model answers with what it has and explicitly states what is missing (US-05 AC4).
- The final `AnswerWithCitations` carries citations of both types where both were used, ordered to match the inline markers.
